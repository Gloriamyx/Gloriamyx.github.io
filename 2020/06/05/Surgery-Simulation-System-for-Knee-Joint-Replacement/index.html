Surgery Simulation System for Knee Joint Replacement

Author: Yuxin Ma Date: June 5, 2020  16:09:33

BACKGROUND:

Utilizing three-dimensional models of medical images offers a direct visual reference for healthcare professionals. The precise reconstruction of the three-dimensional structure of lower limbs is crucial for effective surgical planning.

OBJECTIVE:

To accomplish the reconstruction of the three-dimensional structure through the utilization of continuous Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) and computed tomography (CT) images.

METHODS:

Leveraging toolkits such as the Visualization Toolkit (VTK) and Segmentation & Registration Toolkit (ITK), we employed the Ray Casting method to accomplish the three-dimensional construction of lower limb images based on the provided continuous MRI and CT scans. In our developed system, users can interact with the three-dimensional images by clicking the mouse, enabling them to visualize the images from various perspectives and obtain transaxial, coronal, and sagittal sections. Additionally, our system facilitates the evaluation of the optimal location for prosthesis installation.

RESULTS AND CONCLUSION:

The outcomes indicate that composite volume rendering is a more viable approach for the reconstruction process.
