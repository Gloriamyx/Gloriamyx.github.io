Visual Detection of Cells in Brain Tissue Slice for Patch Clamp System

Author: Yuxin Ma Date: June 7, 2021  10:12:09

BACKGROUND:

Patch clamp, also known as single-channel current recording technology, is a technique that utilizes the sealing of a glass microelectrode to the cell membrane for the detection and recording of ion currents and conductance in ion channels on the patched membrane.

The correlation between the onset mechanisms of neurological diseases such as epilepsy, dementia, and cerebrovascular diseases, and ion channels has been widely recognized. Therefore, patch clamp techniques applied to mouse brain slices can assist in researching corresponding diseases.

As manual patch clamp techniques are challenging to cultivate among technicians, automatic patch clamp technology has become a primary focus of current research. Visual detection of neuronal cells in mouse brain slices is a crucial step for subsequent series of operations.

OBJECTIVE:

To propose a cell detection method based on DIC images, which includes image classification based on image clarity and visual detection of living cells in brain tissue slice based on machine learning.

METHODS:

Collection of DIC (Differential Interference Contrast) images of mouse brain slices

The image datasets used in this project were all collected by team members in the laboratory. We went to the animal resources center to obtain the active mouse brain and then collected moue brain slices.

Brain slice image classification based on image clarity

Combining the gray features and texture features of DIC images, we define a clarity evaluation function based on principal component analysis (PCA). Afterwards, by using K-Means Algorithm, DIC images can be divided into clear class and blurred class.

Machine learning based detection of neural cells in brain slices

We designed a neuron detection process based on sliding window and pre-trained classifier to take cell detection in the selected clear images. We compared the advantages and disadvantages of classification methods based on support vector machine (SVM), AlexNet, VGG, GoogleNet and ResNet and we finally choose GoogLeNet as classifier in the detection process. Then we marked the location of living cells based on the magnitude of probability.

RESULTS AND CONCLUSION:

The precision of image classification is 99.1% in test set, which indicates that it can select the images with clear cell contour from a series of DIC images for subsequent processing. The results show that the detected cells are basically operable living cells. The proposed cell detection technology in this project provides an effective visual tool for fully automated patch clamping.
